Respiratory System

The respiratory system moves air in and out of the lungs and exchanges oxygen and carbon dioxide between air and blood. It also helps regulate acid base balance through CO₂ control.

Main Functions

  • Pulmonary ventilation moves air into and out of the lungs
  • Gas exchange at the alveoli provides oxygen and removes carbon dioxide
  • Helps maintain normal blood pH by changing CO₂ levels
  • Conditions incoming air by warming, humidifying, and filtering

Key Terms

Anatomy and Airflow Pathway

Pathway of oxygen through the respiratory tract

  • Nasal cavity
  • Nasopharynx
  • Oropharynx
  • Laryngopharynx
  • Larynx
  • Trachea
  • Primary bronchi
  • Secondary bronchi
  • Tertiary bronchi
  • Bronchioles
  • Alveolar ducts
  • Alveolar sacs

Conducting Zone vs Respiratory Zone

  • Conducting zone conducts and conditions air but does not exchange gases
  • Respiratory zone is where gas exchange happens between air and blood

Epithelium and Function

  • Trachea and bronchi use pseudostratified ciliated columnar epithelium with goblet cells for mucus and debris removal
  • Bronchioles use simple cuboidal epithelium with smooth muscle to regulate airflow
  • Alveoli use simple squamous epithelium for rapid diffusion
  • Type I cells exchange gas and Type II cells make surfactant

Lungs and Pleura

  • Right lung has 3 lobes and the right bronchus is wider and more vertical
  • Left lung has 2 lobes and has a cardiac notch
  • Parietal pleura lines the chest wall and visceral pleura covers the lung
  • Negative intrapleural pressure helps keep lungs expanded

Common Disorders

  • Pneumothorax is air in the pleural space causing lung collapse
  • Atelectasis is alveolar collapse that reduces gas exchange and lowers oxygen levels
  • Atelectasis prevention includes deep breathing and incentive spirometry

Pneumothorax Treatment

Without equipment

  • Seal an open chest wound using an occlusive dressing taped on three sides

With equipment

  • Chest tube insertion removes air and restores negative pressure

Spirometry Volumes

  • Tidal volume about 500 mL normal breath
  • Inspiratory reserve volume about 3100 mL extra inhaled air
  • Expiratory reserve volume about 1200 mL extra exhaled air
  • Residual volume about 1200 mL air left after max exhale

Mechanics of Breathing

  • Inspiration diaphragm contracts thoracic volume increases pressure decreases air flows in
  • Expiration diaphragm relaxes thoracic volume decreases pressure increases air flows out

Gas Laws

  • Boyle’s law pressure and volume are inversely related
  • Dalton’s law each gas contributes a partial pressure
  • Henry’s law gas dissolves in liquid based on pressure and solubility

Respiratory Control

  • Central chemoreceptors in the medulla respond mainly to increased CO₂
  • Peripheral chemoreceptors in carotid and aortic bodies respond to low O₂ and low pH
  • Medulla and pons regulate reflex breathing and cortex controls voluntary breathing

Acid Base Balance

  • Hyperventilation decreases CO₂ and increases pH which is respiratory alkalosis
  • Hypoventilation increases CO₂ and decreases pH which is respiratory acidosis
  • Bicarbonate buffer system CO₂ + H₂O ⇄ H₂CO₃ ⇄ H⁺ + HCO₃⁻
